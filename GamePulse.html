Math

Fractions, Decimals & Percents

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the top by the bottom. Multiply by 100 for a percent. Example: 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%. To add fractions, get a common denominator first.

Science

States of Matter

The three main states are solid, liquid, and gas. Solids have a fixed shape. Liquids take the shape of their container. Gases spread out to fill all available space. Heat can change states.

Social Studies

U.S. Regions & Geography

The 5 U.S. regions: Northeast, Southeast, Midwest, Southwest, and West. There are 50 states and Washington D.C. is the capital (but not a state). The Mississippi River splits the country.

Science

Food Chains & Ecosystems

Energy flows: Producers (plants use sunlight) → Primary consumers (herbivores eat plants) → Secondary consumers (carnivores eat animals) → Decomposers break everything back down.

Math

Ratios & Proportions

A ratio compares two numbers (e.g. 3:5). A proportion says two ratios are equal: 3/5 = 6/10. Cross-multiply to solve: if a/b = c/d, then a×d = b×c. Used in scaling and recipes!

English

Writing a Great Paragraph

Every paragraph needs: (1) a topic sentence with your main idea, (2) 3–5 supporting details or examples, and (3) a closing sentence. One paragraph = one topic only!

Social Studies

Ancient Civilizations

Key early civilizations: Egypt (pyramids, Nile River), Greece (democracy, Olympics), Rome (roads, aqueducts), Mesopotamia (first writing — cuneiform), China (Great Wall, silk).

Science

Earth's Layers

From center out: inner core (solid iron, super hot), outer core (liquid iron), mantle (hot slow-moving rock), and crust (thin layer we live on). Tectonic plates float on the mantle.
